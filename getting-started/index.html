Getting started

A collection of some resources to get started in quantum computing and quantum algorithms.

Talks

An introductory talk on quantum algorithms by Katalin Friedl, presented at ELTE Summer School in Mathematics.

Books

  • Mika Hirvensalo: Quantum Computing
  • Sándor Imre, Ferenc Balázs: Quantum Computing and Communications: An Engineering Approach
  • Michael A. Nielsen, Isaac L. Chuang: Quantum Computation and Quantum Information

Courses

Another department, BME VIK HIT offers several introductory courses on quantum computing (some also available in English), for example:

  • Kvantuminformatika és -kommunikáció,
  • Introduction to Quantum Computing and Communication,
  • many further ones, as part of the Quantum Information Science minor specialization within the Computer Science Engineering MSc program.

Some advanced level courses given in Budapest include:

  • András Gilyén's Quantum Computing at ELTE,
  • Mihály Weiner's Quantum Probability and Quantum Logic at AIT,
  • Milán Mosonyi's Introduction to Quantum Information Theory at BME TTK.

Further

The Quantum Information National Laboratory is a coordinated national research program that integrates various research groups across Hungarian universities and institutes. Its website provides information on ongoing projects and current events in quantum science in Hungary.
